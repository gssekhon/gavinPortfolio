Photography

Construction

This image I originally took started off by lacking character due to it being overly yellow without any details emphasived. It is easy to pass over this image because after a quick look there is nothing that grabs the viewers eye.

To solve this problem, I needed to make the photo more dramatic. In photoshop, I used primarily the contrast and saturation adjustment tools and turned them up. This increased how vivid the photo was, and it made details pop out. The effect: an overly-dramatic banana that carries a heaver tone than the original banana photo.

Construction

The first picture of the person climbing the wall already has some character and interesting composition to it. It started off a strong image with a story to it. However, the main issue with this photo is the overexposure in the background.

For this photo, instead of trying to correct the background, I took a more unique approach. I took the second photo of the clouds with the earthy tone to them. This tone is important because it matched the original image and makes the editing process smoother. I masked the background in the original image on the top-right, and I cut off the bottom part of the wall. On this mask, I added the clouds, made minor colour adjustments, and feathered the edges to make it look like the wall is actually in the sky. Now, not only is the background problem fixed, but I added to the narrative of the guy climbing the wall by making that wall somehow be in the clouds.

Construction

This photograph of a spoon in bushes had great contrast and subject focus. The shallow depth of field focused intently on the spoon and the details surrounding it. As with the other images, these qualities had the potential to be exemplified.

The biggest and most evident change I made was the change to grayscale. The reasoning for this change is that the green colour of the bushes was more of a distraction to the key elements of the photo. Grayscale images put more emphasis on composition, texture, and contrast, so it was the obvious first move to make in this case. From there, I turned down the contrast to give the bushes smoother looking leaves to them.
